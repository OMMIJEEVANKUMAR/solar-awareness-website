About Solar Energy

Solar energy is a renewable energy source harnessed from sunlight. It can be used for generating electricity through photovoltaic (PV) cells, or for heating via solar thermal systems. Solar power helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions, lower electricity bills, and supports a more sustainable and energy-secure future.

Benefits of Solar Energy

Solar energy offers multiple advantages that contribute to sustainability, cost savings, and a better environment.

Eco-Friendly

Zero emissions. Clean, green power that fights climate change.

Cost Saving

Slash your energy bills with solar-powered electricity.

Energy Independence

Produce your own electricity and reduce reliance on the grid.

Solar Energy Technology

Solar technologies are mainly of two types: Photovoltaic (PV) and Solar Thermal. PV panels convert sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials. Solar thermal collectors absorb solar radiation and convert it into heat for water heating, space heating, or industrial uses. Inverters, smart meters, and batteries are also essential components of a modern solar energy system.

Government Schemes in India

The Indian Government promotes solar energy adoption with several flagship schemes:

  • PM-KUSUM: Solar pumps and grid-connected renewable power plants for farmers.
  • Rooftop Solar Program Phase II: Provides up to 40% subsidy for residential solar installations.
  • Solar Park Scheme: Supports the development of large-scale solar projects.
  • International Solar Alliance (ISA): India-led initiative promoting global solar cooperation.

For more details, visit mnre.gov.in.

India's Solar Growth

India is one of the fastest-growing solar energy producers in the world. The country has already surpassed 70 GW of installed solar capacity and is targeting 280 GW by 2030. With over 40 approved solar parks across 21 states and strong support from public-private partnerships, the future of solar in India is bright.
